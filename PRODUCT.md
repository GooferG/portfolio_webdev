# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Prospective freelance clients and hiring managers evaluating Luiz Meneghim for web development and AI engineering work. Two main personas:

- **The buyer**: small-business owner or PM scoping a website, AI integration, or contract dev. Skims for credibility, scope, and "can this person ship?" Wants to know fit in 30 seconds.
- **The recruiter / engineering lead**: technical reader checking depth — stack, real projects, work history. Reads more carefully, expects clean execution because the portfolio IS the work sample.

Both arrive cold from LinkedIn, GitHub, or referral. Desktop or mobile. Looking to make a hire/contact decision.

## Product Purpose

A personal portfolio that converts visitors into freelance leads and full-time opportunities. The site itself is a competence proof — its craft demonstrates Luiz's ability to ship modern, polished web work. Success = qualified inbound on the contact form and CV downloads.

## Brand Personality

Confident, technical, casual-pro. Three words: **sharp, grounded, modern.** Voice is direct and human ("homie"-tier warmth without losing precision), not corporate or hype-y. The interface should feel like an engineer who cares about craft, not a designer-portfolio showpiece.

Emotions to evoke: trust, "this person ships," subtle delight.

## Anti-references

- **Generic dev-portfolio template** (the dark-bg + teal-accent + scroll-snap landing every junior dev ships). Risk: this site is already in that lane and needs differentiation.
- **Corporate agency site** (over-polished, vague headlines, stock photos).
- **Brutalist / experimental art portfolio** — wrong register for a freelance-client conversion site.
- **SaaS marketing cliché**: hero-metric blocks, identical icon+heading+text card grids, gradient text, glass cards as decoration.
- **AI-slop tells**: purple/blue gradient hero, neon glow on every card, "Available for work" pill that is the only piece of personality.

## Design Principles

1. **The portfolio IS the work sample.** Every element should look like something a client would pay Luiz to build. No template smell.
2. **Confidence over hedging.** "Hire Me" not "Get in touch maybe." Direct copy, decisive layout, no apology for taking space.
3. **Two crafts, one identity.** Web dev and AI engineering live together — design should let both signals land without splitting the brand.
4. **Restraint with one moment of texture.** Mostly clean type and space; one or two earned moments (the shader, the typewriter, the dotted-i) carry the personality. Don't add a third.
5. **Skim-first, depth-on-tap.** Buyers skim, leads dig. Surface the value prop and CTA fast; let project/experience pages reward attention.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- Target WCAG 2.1 AA.
- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` for shader, typewriter, and fade-ins.
- Keyboard navigation must work end-to-end (project cards already use `role="link"` + Enter/Space handlers).
- Color contrast: teal accent on dark bg passes for large text; verify small-text uses on `--fg-muted` / `--fg-subtle` against `--bg-card`.
- Bilingual EN/PT is a personal fact, not a site requirement — site stays English-only.
